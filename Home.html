Steel Products

AngleBar

An angle bar, also known as an angle iron or L-bracket, is a finished metal part shaped like an L with perpendicular legs. These legs may be equal or unequal in length. Angle bars are typically manufactured through hot rolling using either carbon steel or high-strength, low-alloy steel.

FlatBar

Flat bar typically refers to a long, rectangular piece of metal with a flat surface. Its commonly used in construction, manufacturing, and crafting for various purposes like support, framing, and reinforcement.

SquareBar

square bar is a solid steel bar with a square cross-section. It has four equal sides and a smooth surface. These bars are usually manufactured from low-carbon steel, making them cost-effective while maintaining satisfactory strength and ductility.

RoundBar

Roundbar, also known as round bar or rod, is a solid cylindrical piece of metal that is commonly used in various construction, manufacturing, and engineering applications. It is typically made from materials such as steel, stainless steel, aluminum, or brass.

DeformedBar

A deformed bar, also known as a rebar, is a type of steel bar with surface irregularities created to improve its bond with concrete. These deformations provide better anchoring within the concrete, enhancing its structural integrity in construction projects like buildings, bridges, and highways.

ChannelBar

Channel bars are long pieces of metal in the shape of a letter U or letter C, consisting of a wide, flat strip with flanges on either side that form right angles. They got their name from the channel or pathway in between the two flanges and are also known as heavy-duty purlins.

GiSquareTube

GI square tube, also known as galvanized iron square tubing, is a type of tubular steel that has been coated with a layer of zinc to prevent rusting and corrosion. This type of tubing is widely used in construction, industrial, and commercial applications due to its durability and resistance to environmental elements.

Galvanized Plate

A galvanized metal Plate is a steel or iron plate that has been coated with a layer of zinc to protect it from corrosion.

Mild Steel Plate

Mild steel, also known as low-carbon steel, is one of the most widely used forms of steel.

Bi C-Purlins

Black iron C-purlins are structural elements made from untreated steel, commonly used in construction for roofing and wall support

GiCpurlins

Galvanized C-purlins are structural components used in construction, made from steel that has been coated with zinc to prevent rust and corrosion. The C shape of these purlins provides strong support and is commonly used in various building applications.

BiExpandedMetal

Biexpanded metal, also known as double expanded metal, refers to a type of metal mesh that has been processed twice through an expanding machine. The process involves cutting and stretching the metal to create a mesh pattern with larger, more open spaces than standard expanded metal.

BiPerforatedSheets

Bi perforated sheets are metal sheets that have been perforated with two different patterns of holes or two sets of holes at different intervals. This dual perforation can enhance the sheets aesthetic appeal, functionality, or performance in specific applications.

BiPipe

Black iron pipe, also known as black steel pipe, is a type of steel pipe with a dark, matte finish due to iron oxide on its surface, commonly used for gas lines and plumbing due to its strength and corrosion resistance.

BiSquaretube

Black iron square tubing, also known as black steel square tubing, is a hollow, square-shaped metal tube with a dark, matte finish due to the iron oxide on its surface. It’s made from mild steel and is known for its strength and durability.

BoxGutter

A box gutter is a type of rain gutter, typically rectangular in shape, that is used to collect and channel water away from a buildings roof. It is often integrated into the roof design and can be hidden from view, blending seamlessly with the roofline.

Lflashing

L flashing, also known as L-shaped flashing, is a type of building material used to prevent water from penetrating the joints of a building.This flashing is commonly used around windows, doors, and roof edges to divert water away from these areas and prevent leaks.

RegularFlashing

Regular flashing refers to a material, usually metal , installed around areas of a building where the roof meets a vertical surface or at joints to prevent water infiltration. It is commonly used around chimneys, skylights, roof valleys, and along the edges of roofs.

Ridgeroll

Ridge roll, also known as ridge cap or ridge roll flashing, is a type of flashing installed along the ridges of a roof. Its primary function is to cover the gap between roof slopes at the ridge to prevent water, snow, and debris from entering the roof while allowing proper ventilation.

WallFlashing

Wall flashing is a type of flashing used to prevent water from penetrating the junctions where a wall intersects with other building elements, such as roofs, windows, doors, and chimneys. It is typically installed in areas where there is a potential for water infiltration due to the intersection of different surfaces.

CarryingChannel

A carrying channel, also known as a furring channel or hat channel, is a type of metal framing component used in construction. It is typically used to provide support for ceiling systems, wall systems, and other structures.

MetalFurring

Metal furring is a construction technique where thin metal strips or channels are used to create a framework. This framework helps to level out surfaces and provides a base for attaching materials like drywall or plasterboard.

MetalStud

A metal stud is a type of framing used in building construction. Its made from metal, usually steel, and is used to create the skeleton of walls and ceilings. Metal studs are strong, lightweight, and resistant to things like fire, moisture, and pests. They provide a solid structure for attaching drywall and other materials, making them a popular choice in both homes and commercial buildings.

WallAngle

A wall angle is the angle formed where a wall meets a horizontal surface, like the floor or ceiling. It’s important in construction to ensure everything is level and properly aligned. This helps when installing things like fixtures, paneling, or support structures to make sure they fit correctly and look good.

SteelDeck

A steel deck is a construction material made from corrugated steel sheets. These sheets are strong and lightweight, and they’re used to support concrete floors or roofs. The wavy, corrugated design makes the steel even stronger, allowing it to hold up heavy loads with less material.

CheckeredPlate

A checkered plate is a type of metal sheet that has a pattern of raised shapes, like diamonds or lines, on one side. This textured surface helps prevent slipping, making it ideal for places where safety and traction are important, such as stairs, walkways, and industrial floors. It’s also used in vehicles and equipment to provide a sturdy, non-slip surface.

ChickenWire

Chicken wire, also known as poultry netting or hexagonal wire netting, is a type of mesh made from thin, flexible, galvanized steel wire with hexagonal gaps. It is commonly used to build inexpensive fencing and enclosures to confine chickens or other small livestock. It is also used in various DIY projects, gardening, and even in construction as a reinforcement material for plaster or stucco.

ColoredSheet

Colored plain sheet typically refers to plain sheets of metal that are coated with a colored finish. These sheets are often made of materials such as steel, aluminum, or other metals, and are used in various applications like roofing, cladding, and decorative purposes. The color coating provides not only aesthetic appeal but also protection against corrosion and weathering.

SlottedAngle

Slotted angle is a versatile structural system made from steel or other metals, featuring regularly spaced slots along its length. These slots allow for easy assembly and adjustment using nuts and bolts, making it popular for constructing shelving, racks, frames, and other storage solutions. The system is highly customizable, enabling users to create various configurations to suit specific needs.

CycloneWire

Cyclone wire, also known as chain-link fencing or wire mesh, is a type of woven fence typically made from galvanized or coated steel wire. It forms a diamond-shaped pattern, providing a strong and durable barrier commonly used for security, enclosing property, sports fields, industrial areas, and more. The wire is twisted and linked together to create a flexible yet sturdy fence that is resistant to weather and wear.

GiCorrugated

GI corrugated sheets are galvanized iron sheets that have been corrugated, or shaped into a wavy pattern, to increase their strength and rigidity. The galvanization process involves coating the iron or steel with a layer of zinc to protect it from corrosion. These sheets are widely used for roofing and siding in residential, commercial, and industrial buildings due to their durability, weather resistance, and cost-effectiveness.

Longspanribtype

The Longspan rib type, or Long Span Rib Roof, is a type of roofing made from galvanized steel. Its designed with a ribbed pattern that provides strength and durability. This roofing style is popular for covering large areas like warehouses and gymnasiums because it can span long distances without needing many supports.

LongspanCorrugated

Longspan Corrugated roofing is a type of roofing material made from galvanized steel. It has a wavy, corrugated design that makes it strong and flexible. This design helps it handle strong winds and makes it suitable for large buildings like warehouses and factories. It’s also cost-effective because it can cover large areas with fewer materials. Overall, it’s a durable and economical choice for roofing big structures.

GiPipe

A GI pipe, or Galvanized Iron pipe, is a steel pipe coated with a layer of zinc to prevent rusting. This zinc coating makes the pipe durable and long-lasting. GI pipes are commonly used in plumbing, water supply systems, construction, and agriculture because they are strong and resistant to corrosion.

Ibar

An I-bar is a type of metal bar that has a cross-section shaped like the letter “I.” It’s similar to an I-beam but usually smaller. These bars are used in construction because their shape gives them a lot of strength, allowing them to support heavy loads efficiently. They’re often used in frameworks and other structural applications where strong support is needed.

Tbar

A T-bar is a metal bar with a cross-section shaped like the letter “T.” Its used in construction and engineering because its shape provides good strength and stability. T-bars are often made from steel or aluminum, making them durable and resistant to corrosion. They are commonly used in frameworks, supports, and various structural applications.

ZBar

Z-Bars are versatile metal profiles shaped like the letter Z, commonly used in construction and manufacturing for their strength and durability. They are ideal for applications such as framing, insulation reinforcement, and mounting signs or cabinets.

Phenolic

A phenolic board is a strong and durable material made by layering paper or fabric with synthetic resin. These boards are highly resistant to moisture, making them perfect for use in areas like bathrooms and kitchens. They are commonly used for flooring, walls, furniture, and cabinetry due to their robustness and longevity.

FicemBoard

FicemBoard, or fiber cement board, is a sturdy building material made from a mix of cement and cellulose fibers. It’s known for its strength and durability, as well as its resistance to moisture, fire, and termites. This makes it a popular choice for walls, ceilings, and partitions in construction projects.

Plywood

Plywood is an engineered wood product made by gluing together thin layers of wood veneer, with the grain of each layer running perpendicular to the previous one. This cross-grain configuration improves the strength and stability of the plywood, making it less prone to warping and splitting compared to solid wood.

Shafting

Shafting refers to the material or components used to make shafts, which are long, cylindrical pieces used to transmit motion and torque in machinery. Shafts are essential components in various mechanical systems, such as motors, engines, and gearboxes, where they connect different parts and facilitate the transfer of power.

ThreadedBar

A threaded bar, also known as a threaded rod, is a long, straight metal bar with threads cut into it along its entire length. The threading allows for nuts and other fasteners to be screwed onto the rod, enabling it to be used in various construction and mechanical applications.

SteelMatting

Steel matting, also known as welded wire mesh or reinforcing mesh, is a type of material used in construction and industrial applications. It consists of a grid of parallel longitudinal wires welded to cross wires at regular intervals, creating a mesh pattern. This structure provides high strength and rigidity, making it suitable for reinforcing concrete structures, building fences, and various other applications where stability and durability are essential.

StainlessSheet

A stainless sheet, or stainless steel sheet, is a flat piece of stainless steel that is typically available in various thicknesses and dimensions. Stainless steel sheets are known for their excellent corrosion resistance, strength, and aesthetic appeal. They are widely used in various industries, including construction, automotive, aerospace, and manufacturing, for applications such as countertops, appliances, architectural cladding, and more.

BarbedWire

Barbed wire is a type of fencing material constructed with sharp edges or points arranged at intervals along the strands. It consists of two or more twisted strands of wire with barbs (sharp points) spaced at regular intervals. Barbed wire is commonly used for agricultural fencing, security fencing, and other applications where controlling livestock movement or preventing unauthorized access is necessary.

Hardware Product

Arrow Point

arrow point typically refers to a type of fence post cap or finial. These decorative elements are often used to add a stylish touch to the tops of fence posts.This also can protect use from intruders.

Blind Rivets

Blind rivets are ideal for joining materials when you can only access one side, making them perfect for projects where the back side is out of reach. They are easy to use and create strong, reliable joints in various applications like automotive and construction.

Bolt & Nut

Bolts and nuts in a slotted angle bar are used to join sections together securely by passing the bolt through the slots and tightening it with a nut.

Wall Clip

A wall clip, often referred to as a “W-clip,” is a vital component used in ceiling installations involving metal furring. It serves to attach the metal furring channels securely to the walls, ensuring the ceiling framework is both stable and level.

Common Nails

Common nails are versatile fasteners widely used in construction and woodworking. They feature a flat head, smooth shank, and sharp point, making them easy to drive into wood and other materials. These nails are typically used for framing, carpentry, and structural work where strong, durable fastening is needed.

Concrete Nails

Concrete nails are tough, hardened steel nails designed specifically for use in concrete and masonry. They have a thick, sturdy shaft and a sharp point, which allows them to penetrate hard surfaces without bending or breaking. These nails are commonly used in construction to attach wood framing to concrete slabs or to secure fixtures to brick or masonry walls.

Corner Plate

A corner plate is a metal bracket used to connect and reinforce the corners of structures made with slotted angle bars. These L-shaped plates have multiple holes that align with the slots in the angle bars, allowing them to be securely fastened with bolts and nuts.

Slotted Angle Foot

A Slotted Angle Foot for slotted angle bars is a base component designed to provide stability and protect the floor. These footings are typically made of durable plastic and fit onto the ends of the vertical slotted angle bars. They help distribute the weight of the structure evenly, preventing damage to the floor and adding stability to the overall framework.

Cutting Disc

A cutting disc is a circular blade used with power tools like grinders or saws to cut through various materials such as metal, stone, or plastic. These discs are designed to spin at high speeds, allowing them to make precise and efficient cuts. Made from different materials depending on their intended use, cutting discs are essential in construction, metalworking, and DIY projects.

Grinding Disc

A grinding disc is a circular abrasive tool used with grinders to smooth, shape, or remove material from surfaces. These discs are made from abrasive grains bonded together, and they come in various sizes and types depending on the material they are designed to work on, such as metal, stone, or concrete. When attached to a grinder, the disc spins at high speeds, allowing it to grind down surfaces efficiently.

Flap Disc

A flap disc is an abrasive tool used with angle grinders for grinding, blending, and finishing metal and other surfaces. It consists of multiple overlapping layers, or “flaps,” of abrasive material attached to a central hub. As the disc is used, these flaps wear down, continuously exposing fresh abrasive material, which ensures a consistent cut and finish throughout the disc's life.

SandPaper

Sandpaper is an abrasive material used for smoothing, polishing, or cleaning surfaces. It consists of a backing material, such as paper, cloth, or sponge, with abrasive grains glued to one side. These grains, made from materials like aluminum oxide, silicon carbide, or garnet, provide the rough surface needed for sanding tasks. Sandpaper comes in various grit sizes.

Sandflex

Sandflex refers to a type of bi-metal saw blade commonly used for cutting metal. These blades are known for their durability and flexibility, making them ideal for demanding cutting tasks. Sandflex blades are typically used in hacksaws, sabre saws, and power saws, and they can cut through various materials, including metal, wood, and plastics.

Cylindrical

A cylindrical hinge, also known as a barrel hinge or pivot hinge, is a type of hinge that allows two parts to rotate around a central axis, typically in a cylindrical or barrel-shaped housing. These hinges are often used in applications where a clean, compact design is needed, as the hinge mechanism is largely concealed within the cylindrical structure.

Drill Bit

A drill bit is a tool used to create precise holes in various materials, like wood, metal, plastic, and concrete, by attaching it to a drill that spins the bit into the surface. Drill bits come in a range of shapes and designs, each tailored for specific jobs: some are general-purpose, while others are specialized for tougher materials like masonry or for creating large-diameter holes.

Dyna Bolt

A Dynabolt is a type of fastener used in construction to securely anchor objects to concrete or masonry surfaces. It consists of a threaded bolt with an expansion sleeve that expands when the bolt is tightened, creating a strong grip within the drilled hole.

LogScrew

A log screw, also known as a lag screw or lag bolt, is a heavy-duty fastener used primarily in wood construction. These screws are designed to create strong, secure connections in wood, often used for joining large pieces of timber or attaching heavy objects to wood structures.

ExpansionShield

An expansion shield is a type of anchor used to secure objects to materials like concrete, brick, or hollow walls. It works by expanding when a screw or bolt is inserted and tightened, creating a strong grip within the material. This makes it ideal for securing heavy items in construction and masonry work. The expansion shield is particularly effective in softer materials, providing a reliable and sturdy hold.

Epoxy Primer

An epoxy primer is a type of coating used as a base layer on various surfaces to enhance adhesion and provide protection. It's made from a two-component system consisting of an epoxy resin and a hardener. When mixed, these components create a strong, durable primer that adheres well to surfaces like metal, wood, and concrete.

Red Oxide

Red oxide metal primer is a type of coating used to prepare and protect metal surfaces before applying the final paint layer. It's an alkyd-based primer that contains red iron oxide, which helps prevent rust and corrosion on steel and iron surfaces. This primer is particularly effective for both interior and exterior metal applications, providing a strong base that enhances the adhesion and durability of the topcoat.

Metal Text Screw

A Metal Tex Screw, also known as a Tek Screw, is a self-drilling fastener designed for securing metal to metal or metal to wood. These screws have a drill bit end that allows them to create their own hole as they are driven into the material, eliminating the need for pre-drilling. This makes them highly efficient and convenient for construction and manufacturing applications where speed and precision are important.

Roofing Nail

A roofing nail, particularly one with an "umbrella-like" shape, has a large, rounded head that resembles an umbrella. This design offers excellent coverage and helps hold down roofing materials more effectively, preventing them from being lifted or damaged by wind. The umbrella shape provides extra stability and minimizes the risk of tearing around the nail hole, making it ideal for materials like asphalt shingles.

Text Screw Adapter

A Textscrewadapter is a specialized accessory used to connect screws to power tools, particularly drills or screwdrivers, allowing for a quick and efficient way to drive screws into various materials. It works by securing the screw in place so the user can operate the tool at different angles or in hard-to-reach spots without worrying about the screw slipping.

Wood Text Screw

A wood Text screw is a type of self-drilling screw designed for fastening wood to metal or metal to wood without needing a pilot hole. "Text" screws typically have a pointed end that can drill through thin metal sheets, while their threads allow them to grip securely into wood as well.

Insulationfoam

Insulation foam is a material used to reduce heat transfer and improve energy efficiency in buildings. It's typically made from polyurethane or polystyrene and comes in various forms, such as spray foam, rigid foam boards, and foam panels. Insulation foam is applied to walls, roofs, and floors to create a barrier that helps maintain indoor temperatures, reduce energy costs, and enhance comfort.

Gi Wire

GI wire, short for Galvanized Iron wire, is a type of steel wire that has been coated with a layer of zinc through a process called galvanization. This zinc coating helps protect the wire from corrosion and rust, making it durable and long-lasting. GI wire is commonly used in various applications, including construction, fencing, and binding materials together.

Black Screw

A "Blackscrew," commonly referred to as a gypsum or drywall black screw, is a type of fastener designed primarily for attaching drywall to metal or wood framing. These screws typically have a phosphate-coated, black surface, which gives them a corrosion-resistant finish ideal for indoor construction.

Window Handle

A window handle is a component attached to a window that allows you to open, close, and secure it. These handles come in various designs and mechanisms, such as lever handles, crank handles, and locking handles, each suited for different types of windows like casement, sliding, or awning windows.

Window Hinges

Window hinges are the hardware components that allow windows to open and close smoothly. They are typically attached to the window frame and the sash (the part of the window that moves). Depending on the type of window, there are different kinds of hinges, such as butt hinges, friction hinges, and pivot hinges.

Paint Thinner

Paint thinner is a solvent used to dilute oil-based paints and clean up after painting. It helps to reduce the viscosity of the paint, making it easier to apply smoothly and evenly. Additionally, paint thinner is effective for cleaning brushes, rollers, and other painting tools, as well as removing paint spills and splatters.

Lacquer Thinner

Lacquer thinner is a solvent used to thin lacquer-based paints and clean up after their application. It's a powerful mixture of solvents, including acetone, toluene, and methanol, which makes it effective at dissolving lacquer and other tough substances. Lacquer thinner is also used for cleaning brushes, spray guns, and other painting tools, as well as removing adhesives and residues.

Paint Brush

A paintbrush is a tool used for applying paint or other coatings to surfaces. It consists of a handle, usually made of wood or plastic, and bristles, which can be made from natural or synthetic materials.

Mini Roller

A Miniroller is a compact version of a paint roller, designed for painting small or hard-to-reach areas where a standard roller is too large. It's perfect for detailed tasks like painting trim, corners, or narrow surfaces and is often used on furniture, doors, or cabinets to achieve a smooth finish.

Pillow Block

A pillow block, also known as a plummer block, is a type of bearing housing that provides support for a rotating shaft with the help of compatible bearings and various accessories. The housing is typically made of cast iron or steel and is designed to be mounted on a surface, such as a machine frame or base.

Pioneer Elastoseal Pistilito

Pioneer Elastoseal is a high-grade waterproofing sealant commonly used for roof repairs and other sealing applications.

Vulcaseal

Vulcaseal is a popular elastomeric sealant used for sealing, patching, and filling leaks, cracks, holes, and gaps in various materials. It's a one-component sealant that's easy to apply and spread over surfaces like corrugated metal roofing, gutters, downspouts, and plumbing pipes. Vulcaseal is known for its durability and flexibility, making it ideal for both household and construction applications.

Chalkstone

Chalkstone, also known as soapstone or soft stone, is a type of marker used for marking on metal surfaces. It's commonly used in construction and metalworking to create clear, visible lines that guide cutting, welding, or other processes. The soft, smooth texture of chalkstone allows it to write easily on metal without damaging the surface, making it an essential tool for precise and accurate markings.

Steel Brush

A steel brush, also known as a wire brush, is a tool used for cleaning, removing rust, paint, and other debris from surfaces. It consists of a handle, usually made of wood or plastic, and bristles made of steel wire.

Swivel Clamp

A swivel clamp is a type of scaffolding clamp used to connect two pipes at any angle. This flexibility makes it ideal for creating complex scaffolding structures that require various angles and adjustments. Swivel clamps are made of durable materials like galvanized steel, ensuring they can withstand heavy loads and harsh conditions.

Welding Glass

Welding glass is a type of protective eyewear designed to shield the eyes from the intense light and harmful ultraviolet (UV) and infrared (IR) radiation produced during welding. It typically features dark-tinted lenses that filter out these harmful rays, preventing eye damage such as welder's flash or arc eye.

Welding Rod

A welding rod is a piece of metal or alloy that is used in the welding process to join two pieces of metal together. It serves as a filler material that melts and fuses with the base metals, creating a strong bond once it solidifies. Welding rods come in different types, depending on the welding process and materials being joined.

Sliding Door Track

A sliding door track is an essential part of a sliding door system, providing the pathway along which the door moves. Typically made from durable materials like aluminum or steel, these tracks ensure smooth and efficient operation.

Sliding Door Track Roller

A sliding door track roller is a key component in a sliding door system, allowing the door to glide smoothly along the track. These rollers are typically made from materials like nylon or steel, with nylon being quieter and steel offering greater durability.

BI Elbow

A BI elbow, or black iron elbow, is a type of pipe fitting used to change the direction of flow in a piping system. These elbows are typically made from black iron, a material known for its durability and resistance to corrosion. They come in various angles, such as 45 degrees or 90 degrees, to suit different piping configurations. BI elbows are commonly used in plumbing, heating, and gas systems due to their strength and reliability.

Tox

"TOX" often refers to a brand of high-quality construction fasteners and anchors. These products are used to securely attach materials to various surfaces, such as concrete, brick, or drywall. TOX fasteners are known for their durability and reliability, making them a popular choice among professionals in the construction industry.

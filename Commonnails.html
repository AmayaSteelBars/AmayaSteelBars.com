Description

Common nails are versatile fasteners widely used in construction and woodworking. They feature a flat head, smooth shank, and sharp point, making them easy to drive into wood and other materials. These nails are typically used for framing, carpentry, and structural work where strong, durable fastening is needed. Available in various lengths and thicknesses, common nails are usually made of steel, though stainless steel versions are also available for added corrosion resistance. Their design and strength make them a staple in many building projects, providing reliable and straightforward fastening solutions.
